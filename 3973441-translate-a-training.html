Your platform is aimed at an international audience?

After having activated internationalization on your platform, you can then translate the content of your training courses.

Normal access to the management of training translations is done via the Training management screen, by clicking on the Manage Translations button :

It is located below the training data sheet in the top left corner of the screen. You then access the Translation Management Page :

The original language of the training (in this example English) is presented at the top of the list with a progress of "translation" up to 100%.

Note: when creating a training, you must determine the language in which the course is created ("source" language). It will no longer be possible to change the original language (or source language) of the training once you have started to translate the training.

In the example above, the Spanish translation has not started and therefore it is not published. As soon as the translation is finalized, it will be possible to publish this version by clicking in the corresponding checkbox.

Add a translation

To start a translation in a new language, click on + Add translations and choose, from the pop-up that appears, the language of your choice. You can even choose multiple languages ‚Äã‚Äãto enable multiple translations at the same time.

The Rise Up platform allows you to translate your trainings and to export a file including the training in the original language that you can have translated externally before re-importing the file once the translation is done.

> The "Instructions" tab will give you tips for using the file properly;

> The "Data" tab contains the details of the structure of your content, the content in your original language (default language) and a column to complete to integrate your content once translated.

Element: type of element to translate: training, module, stage or session;

Attribute: detail of each element;

Default language: content on the default language to be translated;

Translated language: fill in this column with the desired translation;
